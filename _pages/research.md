## Ongoing Research Projects

### Kenya Reference Genome Initiative
**Role:** Principal Investigator | **Period:** 2024-present | **Organisation:** [CMB Genomics](https://www.cmbgenomics.org)

Population-scale genomic study generating 30x whole genome sequencing data from 1,000 Kenyan adults across all 47 counties and 46 ethnolinguistic groups. Part of the broader East Africa Genome Reference Initiative. Pilot data from 15 genomes has identified over 18 million variants, 9.23% novel to gnomAD v4.1, with 100% of participants carrying clinically actionable pharmacogenomic variants.

---

### The Africa Cancer Atlas
**Role:** Co-Investigator | **Period:** 2022-present | **Organisation:** Yemaachi Biotech

Multi-country cancer genomics programme characterising the molecular landscape of cancers across African populations. Multi-site study with clinical sites across the African continent. Frank serves as Director of Commercial Projects at Yemaachi Biotech, coordinating commercial partnerships and contributing to the scientific programme.

---

### East African HLA and Immunogenetics Atlas
**Role:** Co-Investigator | **Period:** 2024-present | **Organisation:** CMB Genomics

Sequencing-based HLA typing (Class I and Class II) and KIR diversity analysis across East African populations. Building allele frequency and haplotype resources for vaccine design, transplant immunology, and drug hypersensitivity.

---

### Pharmacogenomics of East African Populations
**Role:** Principal Investigator | **Period:** 2024-present | **Organisation:** CMB Genomics

Characterising pharmacogenomic variant frequencies across East African ethnolinguistic groups. Star allele calling and metabolizer phenotype distributions for CYP2D6, CYP2C19, CYP3A5, DPYD, TPMT, UGT1A1, and SLCO1B1. Pilot data shows a mean of 4.6 actionable genes per individual.

---

### Pathogen Genomics and Public Health Laboratory Strengthening
**Role:** Co-Principal Investigator | **Period:** Ongoing | **Partner:** National Public Health Laboratories Service (NPHLS)

Supporting generation of genetic data for selected pathogens with a focus on antimicrobial resistance and emerging outbreak surveillance. Cataloguing genomic data from public health laboratory programmes and coordinating submission to global genomic databases. Strengthening the integration of genomic sequencing into routine public health laboratory workflows.

---

### Validation of Point-of-Care Device for STI Testing
**Role:** Investigator | **Period:** Ongoing | **Partners:** Jhpiego (Johns Hopkins affiliate), funded by the Gates Foundation

Evaluating a point-of-care diagnostic device developed by a US-based startup for sexually transmitted infection testing in Kenyan clinical settings. Assessing sensitivity, specificity, and operational feasibility.

---

### Vaginal Microbiome Dynamics in Women with Heavy Menstrual Bleeding
**Role:** Co-Investigator | **Period:** Ongoing | **Partners:** African Population and Health Research Center (APHRC)

Characterising the vaginal microbiome in women presenting with heavy menstrual bleeding. Pilot grant from APHRC to generate preliminary data for larger grant applications. PhD project for Nasteha Adan Issack.

---

### Self-Sampling Approaches for STI Screening
**Role:** Co-Investigator | **Period:** Ongoing | **Funder:** African Population and Health Research Center (APHRC) | **Location:** Nairobi, Kenya

Community-based recruitment of women through communities and chemists for self-sampling STI screening using a four-plex STI panel. Evaluating acceptability, feasibility, and diagnostic performance of self-collection methods.

---

### HPV Screening in Women Living with HIV
**Role:** Co-Investigator | **Period:** Ongoing | **Location:** Meru and Kilifi counties, Kenya

Molecular characterisation of high-risk HPV genotype prevalence and distribution among women living with HIV and women of reproductive age. Informing targeted vaccination and screening strategies.
