## Ongoing Research Projects

### East Africa Genome Reference Initiative
**Role:** Principal Investigator | **Period:** 2024-present | **Funder:** [Multiple sources]

A multi-country programme generating 30x whole genome sequencing data from nationally representative cohorts across East Africa. The first national programme targets 1,000 adults across all 47 counties and 46 ethnolinguistic groups in Kenya. Pilot data from 15 genomes has identified over 18 million variants, 9.23% novel to gnomAD v4.1.

**Collaborators:** [CMB Genomics](https://www.cmbgenomics.org) research network

---

### East African HLA and Immunogenetics Atlas
**Role:** Co-Investigator | **Period:** 2024-present

Sequencing-based HLA typing (Class I and Class II) and KIR diversity analysis across East African populations. Building the most comprehensive allele frequency and haplotype resource for the region, with applications in vaccine design, transplant immunology, and drug hypersensitivity.

---

### Pharmacogenomics of East African Populations
**Role:** Principal Investigator | **Period:** 2024-present

Characterising pharmacogenomic variant frequencies across ethnolinguistic groups in East Africa. Star allele calling and metabolizer phenotype distributions for clinically relevant genes (CYP2D6, CYP2C19, CYP3A5, DPYD, TPMT, UGT1A1, SLCO1B1). Pilot findings show 100% of participants carry actionable variants, with a mean of 4.6 genes per individual.

---

### Antimicrobial Resistance Surveillance in Livestock-Wildlife Interface
**Role:** Co-Investigator | **Period:** 2020-present

Genomic characterisation of antimicrobial resistance in *E. coli* and other Gram-negative bacteria from camel production systems in Northern Kenya. Whole genome sequencing of beta-lactamase-producing isolates. Collaborative One Health approach.

**Collaborators:** Institute of Primate Research, KALRO, George Mason University

## Past Research Projects

### Cancer Genomics in Kenya (Yemaachi Biotech)
**Role:** Country Director | **Period:** 2022-2025

Established and coordinated cancer genomics operations in Kenya. Built clinical site networks and managed partnerships with hospitals, oncologists, and pathologists for multi-site cancer genomics research. Contributed to the Africa Cancer Atlas initiative.

---

### HIV Drug Resistance Testing Programme
**Role:** Laboratory Manager | **Period:** 2016-2021 | **Institution:** University of Nairobi

Launched a Sanger DNA sequencing service for HIV drug resistance testing. Managed laboratory operations using Sanger and Oxford Nanopore technologies. Part of the Centres of Excellence in HIV Medicine Project.

---

### COVID-19 Laboratory Capacity Building
**Role:** Consultant | **Period:** 2020-2021

Coordinated the establishment of SARS-CoV-2 PCR testing capacity across 15 laboratories in Kenya during the COVID-19 pandemic.

---

### Malaria Host-Parasite Interactions
**Role:** PhD Student / Early Stage Researcher | **Period:** 2009-2014 | **Institution:** University of Oxford / KEMRI-Wellcome Trust

Investigated host adhesion phenotypes for *Plasmodium*-infected erythrocytes and platelet-mediated clumping. Marie Curie Initial Training Network fellowship.

---

### High-Risk HPV Genotyping in Kenyan Women
**Role:** Co-Investigator | **Period:** 2022-present

Prevalence and genotypic diversity of high-risk HPV among women living with HIV and women of reproductive age in Meru and Kilifi counties, Kenya. Molecular characterisation of circulating HPV genotypes to inform vaccination and screening strategies.
