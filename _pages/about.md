I am a genomics researcher based in Nairobi, Kenya. I hold a PhD in Clinical Laboratory Sciences from the University of Oxford, where I investigated host adhesion phenotypes for *Plasmodium*-infected erythrocytes and platelet-mediated clumping at the KEMRI-Wellcome Trust Research Programme.

I am an Associate Professor in Molecular Diagnostics at [Meru University of Science and Technology](https://www.must.ac.ke) and the founder of [CMB Genomics](https://www.cmbgenomics.org), a public benefit organisation generating population-scale genomic datasets and building scientific capacity across East Africa.

## Research Interests

My current work focuses on population genomics, pharmacogenomics, immunogenetics, and multiomics in East African populations. I lead the East Africa Genome Reference Initiative, a multi-country programme generating 30x whole genome sequencing data from nationally representative cohorts. Our pilot data from 15 East African genomes identified over 18 million variants, 9.23% of which are novel to gnomAD v4.1, with 100% of participants carrying clinically actionable pharmacogenomic variants.

My research spans:

- **Pharmacogenomics** across East African ethnolinguistic groups and implications for drug safety and dosing
- **HLA and KIR diversity** and applications in vaccine design, transplant, and reproductive immunology
- **Disease cohort development** for cardiometabolic, reproductive, renal, and neuropsychiatric conditions
- **Multiomics integration** across genomics, transcriptomics, epigenomics, proteomics, and metabolomics
- **Cancer genomics** and molecular diagnostics in African populations
- **Antimicrobial resistance** surveillance and laboratory systems strengthening

## Career

I currently serve as Associate Professor in Molecular Diagnostics in the Department of Medical Laboratory Sciences at Meru University, where I teach genomics, bioinformatics, and molecular diagnostics at undergraduate and postgraduate levels.

Previously, I served as Country Director for Yemaachi Biotech in Kenya (2022-2025), where I established and coordinated cancer genomics operations, built clinical site networks, and managed partnerships with hospitals and pathologists across the country. Before that, I spent five years at the University of Nairobi's Centres of Excellence in HIV Medicine, where I launched a Sanger DNA sequencing service and managed laboratory operations for infectious disease and drug resistance testing. During the COVID-19 pandemic, I coordinated the establishment of SARS-CoV-2 PCR testing capacity across 15 laboratories in Kenya.

Through CMB Genomics, I lead efforts to build genomics and bioinformatics capacity across East Africa, develop biotech ecosystem infrastructure, and work with government agencies to integrate genomic medicine into public health systems.
