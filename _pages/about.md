I am a genomics researcher and bioentrepreneur based in Nairobi, Kenya. I hold a DPhil in Clinical Laboratory Sciences from the University of Oxford, with a background in infectious diseases and host-pathogen interactions.

I am the CEO and Chief Scientific Officer of [Continental Molecular Biosystems](https://continentalmolecular.bio), a genomics and multiomics company generating datasets and disease cohorts from East African and Horn of Africa populations. I am also the founder of [CMB Genomics](https://www.cmbgenomics.org), a public benefit organisation focused on population-scale genomics research and scientific capacity building across the region.

## Research Interests

My current work focuses on population genomics, pharmacogenomics, immunogenetics, and multiomics in East African populations. I lead the East Africa Genome Reference Initiative, a multi-country programme generating 30x whole genome sequencing data from nationally representative cohorts across the region. Our pilot data from 15 East African genomes identified over 18 million variants, 9.23% of which are novel to gnomAD v4.1, with 100% of participants carrying clinically actionable pharmacogenomic variants.

I am particularly interested in:

- **Pharmacogenomics** across East African ethnolinguistic groups and implications for drug safety
- **HLA and KIR diversity** and applications in vaccine design, transplant, and reproductive immunology
- **Disease cohort development** for cardiometabolic, reproductive, renal, and neuropsychiatric conditions
- **Biotech ecosystem development** and scientific workforce training in East Africa

## Background

Prior to founding Continental Molecular and CMB Genomics, my research focused on malaria host-pathogen interactions, HIV drug resistance, antimicrobial resistance surveillance, and molecular diagnostics. I have built partnerships with biopharma and genomics organisations across the continent, with access to clinical networks across East Africa and the Horn of Africa.
